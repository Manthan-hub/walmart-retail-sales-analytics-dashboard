# Walmart Retail Sales & Performance Analytics Dashboard

An executive-style Tableau dashboard analyzing historical Walmart retail sales data from **February 2010 to November 2012**. This project transforms raw sales data into a decision-support dashboard focused on **sales performance, seasonality, macroeconomic context, and year-over-year business trends**.

## Live Dashboard
[View the interactive Tableau dashboard](PASTE_YOUR_TABLEAU_PUBLIC_LINK_HERE)

## Project Overview
Retail leaders need fast, reliable visibility into performance trends, seasonal demand shifts, and external factors that may influence revenue. This dashboard was built to answer a practical business question:

**How can Walmart’s historical sales patterns be translated into clear, decision-ready insights for performance monitoring and planning?**

Using Tableau, I developed an interactive dashboard that highlights:
- overall business performance through KPI cards
- monthly sales trends across multiple years
- the relationship between CPI and weekly sales
- annual performance comparisons for strategic review

## Business Objective
The objective of this project is to create a dashboard that allows business stakeholders to:
- monitor overall retail sales performance
- identify seasonal sales peaks and slower periods
- compare year-over-year performance trends
- assess whether inflation-related indicators such as CPI appear to influence sales
- support data-driven planning and commercial decision-making

## Dataset
The dashboard uses historical Walmart sales data covering **February 2010 to November 2012**. The dataset included weekly sales values and supporting economic indicators used for trend and comparison analysis. The cleaned workbook used in this repo is:

- `Walmart_Store_Sales_Clean.xlsx`

## Dashboard Features
### 1. KPI Cards
The dashboard presents headline metrics for quick executive review:
- **Total Sales**
- **Average Weekly Sales**
- **Maximum Weekly Sales**

These KPIs provide an immediate snapshot of business scale and performance.

### 2. Monthly Sales Trend
A line chart compares monthly sales performance across years to reveal:
- seasonal sales spikes
- recurring monthly demand patterns
- relative strength of each year’s performance

### 3. CPI vs Sales Analysis
A scatter plot visualizes the relationship between **Consumer Price Index (CPI)** and average weekly sales, helping assess whether macroeconomic conditions may correlate with demand behavior.

### 4. Total Sales by Year
A year-level comparison highlights relative annual performance and supports high-level strategic review.

## Key Insights
- **Strong seasonality is visible**, with year-end months showing the highest sales levels, indicating predictable holiday-driven demand.
- **Monthly trend comparisons make year-over-year patterns easy to identify**, allowing stakeholders to distinguish stable performance from exceptional peaks.
- **Annual sales comparisons help benchmark performance across time**, supporting planning and executive reporting.
- **The CPI vs sales view adds broader business context**, helping frame performance beyond internal operational metrics alone.

## Business Recommendations
Based on the dashboard structure and observed patterns, the following actions would be worth exploring in a real business setting:
1. **Strengthen seasonal planning** for peak-demand periods, especially around year-end.
2. **Benchmark high-performing months** to identify promotional, operational, or macroeconomic drivers.
3. **Use year-over-year comparisons** to improve planning discussions and forecast assumptions.
4. **Expand the dashboard** with profit, margin, store-level, and category-level views for more operational depth.
